# The People's First Amendment Press Card

**Open-source, self-issued press credential cards for independent journalists.**

A printable, wallet-sized reference card citing the First Amendment and key court cases affirming the right to record police and practice journalism in public spaces. No fees. No membership. No gatekeeping.

> ⚠️ **Important**: This is **not** a government-issued credential, law enforcement badge, or legal document. It does not grant, create, or replace any legal rights. It is an open-source reference tool for journalists and citizens exercising existing constitutional protections. Not legal advice.

## 🖨️ How to Print

### Home/Office Printing
1. Download the PDF from [Releases](../../releases)
2. Print on **cardstock** (65–110 lb / 176–300 gsm recommended)
3. In print settings: **Actual Size** or **100% scale** (do NOT "Fit to Page")
4. Print **double-sided**, flipping on the **short edge**
5. Cut along trim lines
6. **Laminate** for durability (optional but recommended)

### Professional Print Service
- Upload the PDF directly to your printer (Vistaprint, Moo, local shop, etc.)
- Request: **Cardstock, 16pt or thicker**, with **matte or gloss lamination**
- The file includes bleed and crop marks for commercial printing

### Specifications
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Final Size | 2.125" × 3.375" (standard wallet card) |
| Format | Tri-fold (3 panels per side) |
| Resolution | 300 DPI |
| Color | RGB (modern printers auto-convert); CMYK version available in source |
| Bleed | 0.125" included |

## 📋 What's on the Card

**Front**
- "Issued under authority of the First Amendment, United States Constitution, ratified December 15, 1791"
- Photo field
- Name field
- "Independent Press / First Amendment Journalist"
- Card number field *(for your personal tracking)*

**Inside Panels**
- **Panel 1**: Full First Amendment text + "No federal, state, or local law requires a press pass to practice journalism"
- **Panel 2**: Key court cases affirming journalist rights:
  - *Glik v. Cunniffe* (1st Cir. 2011)
  - *ACLU v. Alvarez* (7th Cir. 2012)
  - *Turner v. Driver* (5th Cir. 2017)
  - *Irizarry v. Yehia* (10th Cir. 2022)
  - *Smith v. City of Cumming* (11th Cir. 2000)
- **Panel 3**: 4th & 5th Amendment reminders + 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (civil rights remedy)

## 🆔 Card Numbering
This project does **not** operate a central verification database. The "Card Number" field is provided for your personal records or organizational use. Fill it in, leave it blank, or assign your own scheme. You control it.

## 🌍 National Scope
This document is designed as a **national reference tool** rooted in the U.S. Constitution. No state or federal statute supersedes the First Amendment's guarantee of a free press. This card is intended for use anywhere in the United States as a practical reference for exercising existing constitutional rights.
